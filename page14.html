Elien Remery

By nature I am more of an introverted person and I do not mind spending time by myself. This gives me peace and energy. But during the covid-19 lockdowns, I found that there is also such a thing as spending too much time with only yourself. Sitting at home every day, no social contact and not going to school was more difficult and harder than I thought. Friends are a big part of my life anyway, and almost every student lives. During the first lockdown in May, I found comfort in nature, walking with the sun in my face, inhaling the fresh air, gave me strength to move on. That is why nature is an important aspect for my mental state during this pandemic. If I get tired of the four walls around me, I go outside and this energizes me.

Sien Cator

In my opinion the COVID-19 pandemic has been a rollercoaster of emotions. There is this strong feeling of missing out on life. We, as student should need this time to get close to our loved ones. We should be able to discover the world, find new interests. Now, this part of life is waiting for us. Otherwise there is the feeling of warmth and growth which comes from friends and family and the ability to be focused on ourselves and our surroundings. We are able to learn more about our environment because we have more time to think about our responsibilities as a human being. This causes a kind of wellbeing that we didn’t know of in the past. The different feelings need to be balanced. Once in harmony, they can leave us with this feeling of comfort where everyone is looking for.

Julie Peeters 

Covid-19 really changed my life, everyone’s life I guess. In the beginning I didn’t think it would have such a huge impact on my life. I filled my days with school and spending time with my family. Little did I know that the virus would be active this long. Nine months and two lockdowns later and there is still no vaccine. Looks like we will just have to deal with this situation. As a third bachelor student, this situation feels like hell. I need to spend time with my friends, meet new people and have fun. I’m grateful that my family and I are healthy and have a good home, but I also hope that this will soon be over and that everything goes back to normal.

Nisrine Guerti

In light of the COVID – 19 pandemic, we need to look out for our planet and way of living. There is no perspective towards the end of this and our population is desperately waiting for a vaccine. In my opinion, pandemics are a part of our lives. We have the chance to change our way of living. As a population we can find a way to build up our immune system instead of hoping for a vaccine. Eat local and organic, take vitamins, don’t use that much antibacterial hand gel. You still need to be exposed to bacteria to keep your immune system strong. In quarantine you can walk in nature and appreciate the fresh air and drops of sunlight. You need to find a balance in this technologic world.

Marie-Alix Van Godtsenhoven

Corona has changed my life considerably. It has changed my outlook on life. The first lockdown started in March and then everything was different, nobody knew how long we would be locked up and when the schools would open again. I don't think anyone didn't think they would be able to go to school until now. I miss the student life, the nice coffees after class or the cafes, to have a drink with friends. I hope everything will soon be back to the way it used to be, but it will never be like it used to be. After corona the world will look completely different, I am already watching with a frightened heart.
